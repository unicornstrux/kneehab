Changes from v5.7
  • Supine Hip External Rotation with Block added to Glute Activation (all days). Fills the gap between 90/90 Hip Rotations (mobility, dynamic) and Clamshell (gluteus medius, abduction-bias). Targets deep external rotators — piriformis, obturator group, gemelli — via sustained end-range isometric hold. Zero knee compression. Prescription: 3 × 30–45s each side. Progress by adding a light band pulling into internal rotation.
v5.6 → v5.7
  • Three blocks reorganised and renamed for functional clarity. "Posture Block" → "Spinal & Shoulder" (Bird Dog, Superman Hold, Down Dog→Up Dog, WLYT, Floor Slide, Scap Push-Up, Face Pull, L-Sit→Reverse Plank, Shoulder Tap). "Core" gains RKC Plank — now 3 exercises (~4 min). "Glute Activation" reduced to Clamshell only — single focused stimulus.
  • RKC Plank moved from Glute block into Core block where it belongs functionally.
  • L-Sit→Reverse Plank and Shoulder Tap remain in Spinal & Shoulder — position transitions flow better there than in Core.
  • Day A subset: Bird Dog, Superman Hold, L-Sit→Reverse Plank, WLYT, Scap Push-Up, Face Pull. Day B subset: Bird Dog, Down Dog→Up Dog, L-Sit→Reverse Plank, Floor Slide, WLYT, Shoulder Tap, Face Pull. Day C: all 9.
v5.5 → v5.6
  • Dead Bug replaced with RKC Plank (2 × 30s). Same anti-extension intent; higher neural demand via full-body co-contraction (glutes, quads, fists, abs simultaneously). Fits isometric loading model better than submaximal rep work. Hollow Body Hold retained alongside it — different demand profile (rectus/hip flexor dominant vs posterior chain co-contraction).
  • External Shoulder Rotation replaced with Face Pull (band, 2 × 15). Targets the same ER deficit but adds scapular retraction load in one movement — addresses the actual desk-posture failure mode rather than isolating ER in a non-functional pattern. Band anchor at face height, elbows flared, pull to forehead with external rotation at end range.

  • Deep Split Squat replaced with Split Squat Progression. Rear leg extended throughout all phases (stretches rectus femoris under load — direct PFPS driver). Four-rung ladder: front foot elevated + counterbalance (Ph1 Wk1–2) → both feet flat + counterbalance (Ph1 Wk3–4) → both feet flat, no counterbalance (Ph2) → Bulgarian with load (Ph3).

Mobility Block 5 min · daily ▼

Stand facing wall, foot ~10cm from base. Drive knee forward over toes until it touches wall, 2s hold at end range, return. Band anchored at face height. Grip overhand, step back to tension. Pull to forehead — elbows flared high, externally rotate at end range so knuckles point back. Addresses ER deficit and scapular retraction simultaneously. Corrects desk-work internal rotation dominance.

Knee & Lower Body — T+45 (12 min)

Collagen rising into peak window. All connective tissue loading starts here.

Knee & Lower Body 12 min ▼
Slant Board Step Down3×10 pri / 3×8 sec
10cm step (Phase 1). Lower opposite foot over 5 sec, 2s pause at bottom (foot hovering, not touching), return. Hand on wall — progress to unassisted. The 2s pause converts eccentric to eccentric-plus-isometric — directly mimics sustained ski loaded position. Primary skiing exercise.
Short Arc Quad3 × 15 each leg · 2s hold
Seated, leg at ~45° flexion. Extend to full extension, 2 sec hold at top, 3 sec lower. VMO is the only quad head that pulls the patella medially — its mechanical advantage is highest in the final 20° of extension. Wall sit and split squat do not reach this range. Mandatory, not optional.
Wall Sit Isometric6 × 30 sec / 45 rest
60° knee flexion. Dull ache acceptable. Sharp or >4/10 = reduce angle immediately. Start 45° in Weeks 1--2 if needed. Tissue stimulus — not strength training.
Split Squat Progression2 × 8–10 each side
Rear leg extended throughout all phases — not bent to 90°. Extended rear leg stretches rectus femoris under load, directly addressing a primary PFPS driver. 2–3s hold at bottom. Progress through ladder: Ph1 Wk1–2: front foot elevated 10–15cm (same step as step-down), rear leg extended on floor, counterbalance. Ph1 Wk3–4: both feet flat, rear leg extended, counterbalance — reduce elevation gradually. Ph2 entry: both feet flat, rear leg extended, no counterbalance. Ph3: rear foot elevated (Bulgarian) with load.
Lateral Side Step2 rounds × 15 steps
Slight squat, hip tension throughout. Pause and reassert hip position after each step before the next — deliberate load check, not continuous momentum. Add resistance band if available.
Hamstring Slide3 × 8–10 · Day B only
On back, heels on sliders or towel on smooth floor. Drive hips up into bridge, slide heels out slowly (3–5 sec), return. Eccentric-concentric hamstring load — lower peak force than alternatives, appropriate for current phase. Day A only — 48h eccentric recovery required. Significant assistance from arms in early weeks.
Single-Leg Calf Raise — Straight Knee2 × 15 each · 3s down
Stand on one foot, full plantarflexion at top, full dorsiflexion at bottom. 3s eccentric. Targets gastrocnemius. Use wall for balance. Build to unassisted. Supports Achilles and ankle load tolerance for skiing and hiking.
Single-Leg Single-Leg Calf Raise — Bent Knee (Soleus)2 × 12–15 each · 3s down
Knee bent ~30°, same single-leg raise. Bent knee removes gastrocnemius from the load — isolates soleus. Soleus is the primary plantarflexor under knee-flexed load: wall sit, step down, split squat, ski descent, cycling. Previously absent from the program entirely. This is the higher-priority of the two calf raises.

Sleep & GH Protocol

Collagen synthesis peaks during sleep via the GH pulse. The pre-session collagen window is addressed in the daily schedule — the nocturnal synthesis window is equally important and equally controllable.

Duration
7–8h minimum
GH pulse amplitude is sleep-duration dependent. Chronic short sleep suppresses the nocturnal collagen synthesis peak regardless of supplementation.
No food
90 min before sleep
Insulin response from any macronutrient suppresses GH pulse. Any protein meal within 90 min of sleep blunts the overnight synthesis window.
No alcohol
Same mechanism as food
Alcohol suppresses GH secretion and disrupts sleep architecture. Any amount. Not a training day concern only — applies every night.

Phases & Progression

Three phases. Phase 1 is fully prescribed — do not advance until all exit criteria are met. Phase 2 and Phase 3 are outlined; full prescriptions are written on entry.

Phase 1 — Foundation · Weeks 1–4
Weeks 1--2 Early foundation
Split squat: front foot elevated 10–15cm, rear leg extended on floor, significant counterbalance. Do not force depth.
Step down: hand support on wall, 10cm step.
Scap push-up on knees.

Weeks 3--4 Building tolerance
Hamstring slides: progress to single-leg. Increase range of motion — start with partial slide, build toward full extension.

Elliptical OK for Zone 2 if no post-session knee soreness.

Goal
Full ski season without PFPS flare
Descending blue and black runs, sustained ski days, commuting without soreness onset.
Key Rules
01
Mobility is non-negotiable
Daily on all day types including Day C. Hip and ankle stiffness are upstream drivers of knee load.
02
Collagen on load & activity days only
Base days have no loading event that warrants priming.
03
All collagen: 2+ hours after any protein meal
Leucine blunts connective tissue synthesis response.
04
Load day morning sequence is fixed
Reordering disrupts the collagen timing architecture.
05
Bellemans Bike is the collagen target
Everything before it lands the bike at T+57, inside the 60--90 min peak window. Zone 2 commute counts as the Bellemans session — do not stack both.
07
Skin-sourced collagen only
Type I. Bone broth and gelatin are not equivalent.
08
Hamstring slides on Day B only
48h eccentric recovery from Day A. Separation from bike session reduces posterior chain stacking.
09
Knee micro-sessions on Days A & B only
Loaded stimuli — not habits. Excluded from Day C.
10
Tibialis raise, backward walk, dead hang daily
No recovery demand — corrective and decompressive without exception.
11
Buffer rule — base only if tomorrow is activity
Regardless of where it falls in the load schedule.
12
Activity days replace a Day A or B
Do not stack structured knee work on top.
13
Zone 2: swimming or elliptical only — Phase 1
No outdoor cycling as Zone 2. Phase 2+.
14
Zone 1 on Day C is unrestricted
Backward walk, easy bike commute, kayaking, casual swim. Zone 1 commutes do not require collagen and do not displace the Bellemans session.
15
Zone 2 commute replaces Bellemans Bike — not adds to it
Take collagen 45 min before. Run morning routine. Skip T+57 Bellemans session. Monitor next-morning soreness every commute in Phase 1.
16
Bellemans Bike: 10--15 min maximum
Cartilage adaptation window closes at 15 min. This applies to the stationary bike only — commutes are not capped.
16
Pain guide
Dull ache acceptable. Sharp or >4/10 = reduce load or angle immediately.
17
Do not skip mobility
Daily including Day C. Hip and ankle stiffness are upstream causes of knee load.
18
Glute work is not optional
VMO alone will not fix patellar tracking without glute medius.
19
Deep range is the goal
Use whatever assistance needed to reach depth — reduce over time.
Based on Bellemans cyclic loading, Baar connective tissue loading & nutrition, and ATG (Ben Patrick) anterior chain & deep range principles.
