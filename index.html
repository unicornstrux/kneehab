Changes from v7.0 — Floor L-Sit added
  • Floor L-Sit added to optional casual micro-session pool. Seated, heels on floor, hands beside hips, press down through straight arms. 2–3 × 20–30s. Unique contributions not covered elsewhere in the program: active shoulder depression under vertical load (lower trap, serratus anterior in compression pattern vs. shear pattern of Scap Push-Up) and tricep isometric. Partial overlap with hollow body hold (anterior core isometric) and RKC plank (co-contraction) but not redundant — different arm pattern and hip position. Not added to Core block — micro-session placement preferred because isometric adaptation is driven by frequency across the day, not single-session volume. Floor access required. No recovery demand. Progress by reducing heel support height over weeks.
Changes from v6.9 — Scientific review, protocol rebuild, progressive overload
  • Collagen protocol rebuilt. Day-label rules replaced with a single universal rule: 15g + 200mg vit C, 45–60 min before any qualifying mechanical load session, minimum 2h after last protein meal. Explicit load session table added. "No collagen on Day C" rule replaced with loading-event logic — collagen warranted on Day C only if upper body block is included. "Minimize saturated fat" and "no food 90 min before sleep" removed from collagen section — general dietary advice, not collagen-specific.
  • Wall sit duplication resolved. Wall sit appeared in both Knee & Lower Body block (6×30s) and mandatory knee micro-sessions (5×20–30s) — 11 sets total on Days A and B. Main block version removed. Micro-session version retained: 5 sets per session.
  • Lateral Side Step removed. Replaced by Lateral Step-Up (Day A) and Skater Squat (Day B).
  • Lateral Step-Up added — Day A. 15cm box, 3s eccentric, bodyweight Phase 1. Levers: 15cm→20cm→25cm / eccentric tempo / bodyweight→loaded.
  • Skater Squat added — Day B. Start assisted, partial depth. Levers: assisted→unassisted / partial→full depth / standard→3s eccentric / bodyweight→loaded.
  • Cossack Squat added to Phase 2 lever pool. Not in Phase 1. Introduced Phase 2 when deep knee flexion pain-free and single-leg control established.
  • Floor Slide moved to Day C only. WLYT covers same scapular pattern on Days A and B.
  • Straight-knee calf raise reduced to 1 set Days A and B. Soleus already marked higher priority. Full volume returns Phase 2+.
  • RKC Plank and Supine Hip External Rotation with Block retained. Both were reviewed for redundancy and reinstated. RKC Plank = full-body co-contraction, qualitatively different from hollow body. Supine Hip ER = end-range isometric for deep external rotators — diagnosis-specific for PFPS, not replaceable by 90/90 hold.
  • 2×2 Rule added as universal progression gate. ≤2/10 pain during and morning after, two consecutive sessions → pull next lever. Pain >2/10 → hold one full week. Replaces hard phase transitions.
  • Progression levers added to all key exercises. Wall Sit, Step Down, Lateral Step-Up, Skater Squat, Split Squat, Calf Raise, Hamstring Slide, Short Arc Quad, Cossack Squat — each has a defined lever sequence. Program never hard-resets.
  • Phase structure reframed. Phases 1/2/3 retained as landmarks. Phase 1 exit criteria converted to a readiness check — signal to introduce new exercises one at a time, not a program gate. New criteria: minimum 4 weeks, single-leg step-up 15cm, hamstring isometric tolerance, symmetry ≥80%.
  • Hamstring slide tempo and regression added. Eccentric tempo: 4–6s lowering phase. Regression: assisted partial range → full unassisted.
  • Split squat Phase 1 constraints added. Step length moderate, trunk upright, knee at or inside toes, bodyweight only until readiness criteria met.
  • Copenhagen Plank bridge added. Adductor side-lying isometric hold (30s each side) inserted between hip abduction and Copenhagen plank.
  • Dead hang qualifier added. Skip or reduce if any shoulder discomfort or overhead history.
  • Bellemans Bike added to Day C as optional. 10–12 min, hard stop 15 min. Increases cartilage loading frequency to 3× per week. No collagen unless upper body block also completed.
  • Micro-sessions restructured. Short Foot Isometric and Standing Hamstring Isometric Near Extension promoted to mandatory daily. Optional casual pool added: Short Arc Quad Hold, Single-Leg Calf Raise, Wall Sit Short Hold, Shoulder CARs, Neck CARs.
  • Zone 2 timing rule added. Non-morning Zone 2: take collagen 45–60 min before only if ≥2h post last protein and session ends ≥2h before sleep.
  • Spinal & Shoulder block updated: Day A runs 4 exercises (was 6), Day B runs 6 (was 7), Day C runs all 7 (was 9).

General Optional pick 1 ▼
Short Foot Isometric ★1 × 30s each side · barefoot · see progression
Targets intrinsic foot muscles (flexor digitorum brevis, abductor hallucis) for arch stabilisation during ground contact. Relevant for running and hiking — no rigid sole bridges the load path, arch must self-stabilise each footstrike. Not a cycling fix (cycling lateral load is geometric — cleat, wedge, insole). Motor pattern drill, not a strength exercise.

Progression — do not skip phases:
Wk 1–2 · Seated unloaded: sit, foot flat on floor, place thumb under navicular (inner mid-foot bony bump). Spread toes, press ball of foot and heel into floor simultaneously, dome arch without curling toes. Thumb gives biofeedback — feel the small lift. Hold 30s each side. If no lift is felt, the wrong muscles are working.
Wk 3–4 · Standing bilateral: both feet, bodyweight, same dome cue. Harder — gravity now works against the arch. Hold 30s each side.
Wk 5+ · Standing single-leg: one foot, full bodyweight. Approximates mid-stance load during walking and hiking. Hold 30s each side.

Drop when arch holds automatically during step-downs and single-leg balance without conscious cuing. With confirmed load-dependent collapse (arch holds unloaded but drops under bodyweight), expect 6–8 weeks to reliable single-leg hold — not 3–4.
Standing Hamstring Isometric — Near Extension ★3 × 20--30s each leg · 10--15° flexion
Stand facing a wall, slight knee bend (10–15° — barely perceptible, not a squat). Press heel back and into the floor as if trying to drag it toward you. Hold 20–30s. The hamstrings are working isometrically at near-full extension — the functional angle for prolonged standing.

Why this matters: Prolonged standing causes the knee to drift toward passive hyperextension — joint held by ligaments and posterior capsule rather than muscle. This places sustained passive tension on the posterior capsule (the symptomatic attachment site) and increases patellofemoral contact pressure by removing hamstring co-contraction. Weak hamstrings at near-extension cannot prevent this drift. This exercise builds the holding capacity to actively manage knee position during long standing periods — hiking flats, ski lift queues, standing desk, kitchen.

What you should feel: Low-level tension in the posterior thigh, not a hard contraction. This is an endurance isometric, not a strength exercise. If it cramps immediately, the muscle is severely underused at this range — reduce to 10s and build.

No recovery demand. Do at desk, kitchen, anywhere. Pairs directly with the Hamstring Isometric in Flexion — together they cover near-extension (standing) and mid-range (jump landing) in one program.
